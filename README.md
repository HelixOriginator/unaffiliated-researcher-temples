100 Temples for Unaffiliated Independent Researchers

A vision to recognize, preserve, and celebrate the creativity, resilience, and practical intelligence of ordinary people whose innovations emerge from lived experience, necessity, self-reliance, and independent thinking.

Overview

100 Temples for Unaffiliated Independent Researchers is a conceptual initiative proposing the creation of one hundred physical and digital spaces dedicated to documenting and honoring grassroots innovators, independent thinkers, and community problem-solvers.

These "temples" are not religious institutions. They are public monuments to human ingenuity and repositories of practical knowledge developed outside traditional academic, corporate, or governmental structures.

The project seeks to preserve the contributions of individuals whose work often remains invisible despite its real-world impact.

Vision

The project aims to establish a network of one hundred decentralized repositories that:

Recognize the intellectual wealth of ordinary citizens.
Preserve grassroots innovation for future generations.
Validate lived experience as a legitimate source of knowledge.
Celebrate creativity regardless of formal credentials.
Create permanent archives of practical problem-solving.
Why This Matters

Many transformative ideas emerge from scarcity rather than abundance.

Farmers, artisans, teachers, local entrepreneurs, public servants, and marginalized communities frequently develop innovative solutions without institutional support. Yet their contributions are often undocumented or overlooked.

This initiative seeks to:

Preserve endangered local knowledge.
Recognize overlooked innovators.
Encourage peer-to-peer learning.
Build community-centered archives of human creativity.
Core Philosophy

Greater hardship often produces deeper creativity. Necessity can become the most powerful engine of innovation.

The project recognizes that innovation is not exclusive to universities, laboratories, or research institutions.

Knowledge generated through lived experience possesses its own rigor, methodology, and value.

Intellectual Sovereignty

Many independent innovators lack access to traditional intellectual property systems.

This project explores principles from Decentralized Science (DeSci) to:

Establish proof of origin.
Create timestamped records of innovation.
Support attribution.
Strengthen intellectual sovereignty for grassroots creators.
Ethical Principles

The initiative is guided by the following principles:

Respect for human dignity.
Equal recognition for all contributors.
Voluntary participation.
Informed consent.
Non-political operation.
Non-sectarian inclusivity.
Open access documentation.
Community-centered preservation.
Recognition based on contribution, not credentials.
Long-Term Goal

To redefine who society considers an innovator and broaden public understanding of creativity beyond institutional boundaries.

The project seeks to demonstrate that some of humanity's most valuable contributions emerge from everyday people solving real-world problems.
